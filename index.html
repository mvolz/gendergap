Are our editors representative of our readers?

No. There are lots of ways our editors are not representative of our readers. More educated, for one, but one of the really obvious ones is... gender!

Significant Editor Gender Gap

This is a list of surveys that have been conducted to get at the editor gender gap. Please note that these are not corrected surveys but the raw data. For instance Hill and Shaw 2016 used Pew Surveys to correct the 13% figure at the bottom here to 16%. However, even if these are an underestimate of the total number of registered editors, we still have a significant problem as the most prolific users are more likely to be male. So the percentage of total content produced by women is likely even smaller than the total percentage of male editors. Also please note that these are somewhat geographically biased because most of these surveys are done on English Wikipedia, although there are some global south surveys in this chart as well. Welsh Wikipedia actually has a gender bias in the other direction, 55% of registered editors there are female!
< 1/4 of registered Wikipedia editors are female; active editors may be even lower.

Intersectional Gaps

Who else are we missing?
Gender minorities Sexual minorities Racial minorities Religious minorities Non-English speakers Disabled And probably *lots* of others.
So in 2014 someone wrote this blog post asking why women have no time for Wikipedia, and in it they estimated only 2% of Wikipedians are mums. I actually responded and exorciated them for their misuse of statistics (my own estimate was 4%, with MAJOR caveats) but on the other hand they had some great points. One thing they mentioned is that women are producing content elsewhere, just not on Wikipedia. I really started to notice how much missing content there was from mums. At the time my first son was 2 and I had spent a lot of time on babycenter (US version of mumsnet) and then a lot of time in Facebook groups. Even on reddit there's massive amounts of content being produced on childrearing, feeding... just not on Wikipedia.
